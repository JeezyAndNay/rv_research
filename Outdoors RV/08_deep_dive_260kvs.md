## The 260KVS at a Glance

The Blackstone 260KVS is Outdoors RV's **three-slide rear-kitchen couples trailer** with a defining design choice: a panoramic rear window where other manufacturers put overhead cabinets. You get a full-wall view from the kitchen and dining area instead of cabinet storage — a deliberate trade that changes how the rear of the trailer feels and lives.

Three slides total: two in the living area (dinette + sofa/recliner) and a dedicated **wardrobe slide in the bedroom** — rare below 40 feet. That bedroom wardrobe slide is the 260KVS's calling card for extended travel. The layout prioritizes couple comfort and storage over sleeping capacity (sleeps 4 vs. the 260KRS's 5).

Dual entry doors — one at the rear kitchen, one at the front bedroom — mean you can enter from the work side of the campsite through the kitchen and keep trail dirt out of the bedroom entirely.

> **"KVS" designation:** K = rear Kitchen configuration, V = panoramic View window (the defining feature), S = three-Slide layout. This is ORV's code for this specific floor plan combination.

## Layout Description

The 260KVS is a **front-to-rear flow** layout optimized for two people doing extended travel.

**Front — Private Bedroom**
- Queen bed
- Dual wardrobes flanking the bed on each side
- **Wardrobe slide (slide 3)** — extends the bedroom closet laterally, adding real hanging and folding storage rarely available under 40 feet. This slide exists only on the 260KVS in the Blackstone lineup
- Front entry/exit door

**Center — Walk-Through Bath**
- Skylight
- Full bathroom accessible from the bedroom corridor OR the living area — dual-access means no crossing the living area at night
- Roomy shower with ample movement space (noted consistently in owner reviews)

**Living Area — Dual Opposing Slides**
- **Slide 1: Dinette slide** — mountain dinette (table + bench seating, seats 4)
- **Slide 2: Sofa/recliner slide** — residential sofa with built-in recliners
- Swing-out 40" TV mounted between slides, faces living area
- When both slides open, the living section is notably wide for a 32-foot trailer

**Rear — Kitchen with Panoramic View**
- Three-burner cooktop
- LG solid surface countertops (Titanium)
- Microwave
- Built-in **trash chute** — countertop opening drops directly to an exterior baggage compartment accessible from outside without opening interior doors
- **Panoramic rear window** — full-width rear window with NO overhead cabinets above the kitchen. Cooking faces the back of the campsite, not your cabinet fronts. This is the 260KVS's signature detail
- **Dual entry** — rear kitchen door + front bedroom door. Come in from the trail through the kitchen; bedroom stays clean

## 260KVS vs. 260KRS — Head to Head

These two share the same exterior length (32'4") and rear kitchen configuration. The differences are meaningful.

| | 260KRS | 260KVS |
|---|---|---|
| Slides | 2 (dinette + bedroom) | 3 (dinette + sofa + wardrobe) |
| Living area width | 1 slide open | 2 slides open — wider living |
| Bedroom storage | Standard wardrobes | Wardrobe slide — substantially more hanging space |
| Entry doors | Single (front) | Dual (front + rear kitchen) |
| Sleeps | 5 | 4 |
| Dry weight (Titanium) | 8,280 lbs | 8,330 lbs |
| Exterior length | 32'4" | 32'4" |

**The call:** If sleeping 5 matters — 260KRS. If it's 2 people doing extended off-grid travel who want more wardrobe storage, a wider living area, and dual entry doors for a working-outside lifestyle — 260KVS is the stronger layout.

## What Owners Say

**Overall Blackstone series:** 4.5 out of 5 stars, 4.8 on towing, 4.7 on liveability. Consistently earns highest marks on towing behavior and liveability. Five-year owners report the platform holds up in hard-use scenarios.

**Manufacturing support:** The standout differentiator. Outdoors RV answers the phone, routes to the right person, sends parts, and walks through installation patiently. Repeatedly cited by owners as the reason to choose ORV over Indiana manufacturers.

**On the split gray tanks:** The 260KVS runs two 40-gallon gray tanks (80 gal combined). Boondockers note the split matters — one tank fills faster depending on which sink or shower you use most. Manage your dump timing per tank, not just total gray capacity. Some owners put a valve on each tank line to control which fills first.

**On the wardrobe slide:** No model-specific failure reports yet on 260KVS-specific units. However, the Schwintek wardrobe slide failure pattern is documented on the 250RKS — a two-year-old slide requiring full rebuild. Given shared slide hardware across the Blackstone line, verify thoroughly at PDI.

## Known Issues

| # | Issue | Source | PDI Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 1 | **Panoramic rear window seal perimeter** — no overhead cabinet means the full rear wall is window. More window perimeter = more sealant to inspect. Any missed inch = water intrusion into rear kitchen | Blackstone construction observation | Inspect all 4 edges of rear window + both flanking side windows — exterior, full perimeter |
| 🔴 2 | **Wardrobe slide (3rd slide)** — unique to 260KVS. Schwintek failure pattern documented on Blackstone 250RKS wardrobe slide within 2 years. More slides = more mechanical complexity | iRV2 Blackstone owner reports | Run wardrobe slide 5 full cycles. Listen for motor strain, watch for one-sided walking |
| 🔴 3 | **Wheel bearing over-greasing** — documented across Blackstone line. Factory over-greases at assembly; grease migrates to brake drums, causing brake fade or failure | RV Insider Blackstone owner reviews | Request bearing inspection at PDI; plan repack within first 3,000 miles regardless |
| 🔴 4 | **Water tank fill hose** — documented Blackstone series failure. Fill hose disconnects or develops a leak inside the underbelly; floods insulation and underbelly structure | iRV2 + RV Insider Blackstone reviews | Fill fresh tank from city water; inspect underbelly for moisture during the fill |
| 🟡 5 | **Auto-leveling calibration** — common on Titanium delivery units. System raises jacks to incorrect height; battery display disagreement with charge controller | Multiple Titanium owner reports | Full auto-level cycle demo with all 3 slides extended on an unlevel surface; get re-zero procedure in writing |
| 🟡 6 | **Trash chute seals** — built-in feature; exterior baggage door and interior countertop cover must both seal properly. An unsealed chute is a moisture and pest entry point | Design observation | Test exterior door seal; verify interior cap is present and flush |
| 🟡 7 | **Dual entry door seals** — two exterior doors = two perimeter seals to verify. The rear kitchen door is unique to 260KVS; verify it closes and latches squarely and the weatherstrip compresses fully | General Blackstone construction | Both doors: 3 open/close/latch cycles; weatherstrip check; threshold inspection |
| 🟡 8 | **NHTSA Recall 24V813000 applicability** — recall covers 2025: 250RKS, 250RDS, 260KRS, 25DVS. 260KVS was NOT named. However, it shares the same cooktop/furnace proximity design | NHTSA recall 24V813000 | Check VIN at nhtsa.gov before PDI. If no recall found, document the check date in your PDI paperwork |

## Honest Bottom Line

The 260KVS Titanium is the most **couples-optimized** Blackstone on the shortlist. Three slides mean the living area is genuinely wide when set up. The wardrobe slide is a legitimate quality-of-life feature for extended travel — actual hanging closet space in a travel trailer is rare and matters at week 4, not week 1.

The panoramic rear kitchen window is a real differentiator. Cooking while looking at the campsite or mountains instead of overhead cabinet fronts is the kind of detail that compounds over hundreds of nights.

The dual entry keeps mud, gear, and trail dirt from migrating through the bedroom — practical for the dispersed-camping use case being built for.

**Watch:** Three slides is more mechanical complexity than two. The wardrobe slide needs thorough PDI verification and ongoing maintenance. Auto-leveling on a three-slide unit must work correctly — verify with all slides extended, not on a flat dealer lot with slides retracted.

**Tow requirement:** Ram 3500 SRW handles this with headroom. At 8,330 lbs dry, GVWR 11,000 lbs — no constraints.
