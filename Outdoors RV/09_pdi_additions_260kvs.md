# Outdoors RV — PDI Additions: Blackstone 260KVS (Titanium)
*Research date: 2026-06-18*

> **How to use this file:** Run the full generic ORV PDI checklist (`Outdoors RV/02_pdi_checklist.md`) first. Then check the 260KRS section in `07_pdi_additions_250rks_250rds_260krs_25dvs.md` — the 260KVS shares several Blackstone construction points with that model. This file adds PDI items specific to the 260KVS's unique features: the third (wardrobe) slide, panoramic rear window, dual entry doors, split gray tanks, and trash chute.

## ⚠️ NHTSA Recall — Verify Before You Schedule

NHTSA Recall 24V813000 was issued for cooktop flame inversion (stove + furnace running simultaneously). The original recall named 2025 models: 250RKS, 250RDS, 260KRS, 25DVS.

**The 260KVS was not named in the original recall.** However, it shares the same cooktop/furnace proximity design as the named models.

- [ ] **Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov before your PDI appointment**
- [ ] If any open recall appears — require the dealer to close it before delivery, documentation in hand
- [ ] If no recall appears — write this in your PDI paperwork: *"VIN checked at nhtsa.gov on [date] — no open recalls found"*
- [ ] Do not skip this step — the 260KVS design is close enough to the named models that verification is non-negotiable

## Wardrobe Slide (Third Slide) — 260KVS Specific

*The wardrobe slide is the mechanical feature that separates the 260KVS from the 260KRS. Schwintek-style slide motor. A Schwintek failure pattern is documented on the Blackstone 250RKS within 2 years of ownership. Verify thoroughly.*

- [ ] **🔴 Run 5 full cycles (in and out)** — more cycles than a standard slide check. This is a dedicated storage slide, not a living-area slide; it gets verified less thoroughly on typical dealer walk-throughs. Run it 5 times yourself
- [ ] **Listen for motor strain** — any grinding, grunt, or hesitation means the motors or gears are binding. Healthy Schwintek operation is smooth and consistent. Any noise = don't sign until inspected
- [ ] **Watch for one-sided walking** — observe the slide face as it extends. If one side advances faster than the other (the face tilts), the two Schwintek motors are out of sync. This is a dealer adjustment before delivery
- [ ] **Wardrobe door alignment with slide extended** — open and close each wardrobe door with the slide fully extended. Doors should align square, swing freely, and close without drag or binding. Factory alignment on a 3rd slide is a known variable on production trailers
- [ ] **Slide-out seal compression (all 4 edges)** — with wardrobe slide extended, run your hand along all four edges of the slide opening from inside. The rubber seal should be compressed evenly all the way around with no daylight visible anywhere

## Rear Panoramic Window — 260KVS Specific

*The 260KVS's rear window runs the full width of the rear wall with no overhead cabinets above the kitchen. More window = more seal perimeter = more to verify.*

- [ ] **🔴 Full exterior perimeter — all 4 edges of the main rear window**
  - Walk to the rear exterior of the trailer
  - Inspect every inch of sealant around the rear window (top, both sides, bottom)
  - Look for: cracks, gaps, bridging (sealant pulled from one surface), thin spots at corners, or discoloration indicating prior moisture contact
  - The top edge is the highest-risk point — any gap here sends water directly into the rear kitchen wall
- [ ] **Both flanking side windows** — rear windows run on both sides of the kitchen area, not just the center. Inspect each side window's seal perimeter separately
- [ ] **No overhead cabinet means no secondary protection** — on trailers with overhead kitchen cabinets, the cabinet creates a secondary barrier to water above the window frame. The 260KVS has no cabinet here. The sealant is the only defense. Treat it as such
- [ ] **Interior rear wall check** — from inside, look and feel across the full top of the rear window from the inside. Press gently along the top of the wall above the window. Any soft spot, flex, or discoloration (watermark, staining) is a sign of prior intrusion. Do not accept the unit if you find this

## Dual Entry Doors — 260KVS Specific

*Two exterior doors means two sets of door hardware, seals, and alignment to verify.*

**Rear Kitchen Door (trail entry door):**
- [ ] Open, close, and latch — 3 full cycles. Latch should engage cleanly and positively each time
- [ ] No binding, no dragging in the frame at any point in the swing arc
- [ ] Deadbolt (if equipped) — full throw and retract without force
- [ ] Weatherstrip — close the door and run your hand around the full interior perimeter. The strip should be compressed consistently all the way around. Any gap = cold, moisture, and insects
- [ ] Threshold / bottom seal — check the bottom weather strip. This sees the most foot traffic and is the most commonly compressed or misaligned seal at delivery

**Front Bedroom Door:**
- [ ] Same checks as rear door — 3 cycles, latch function, weatherstrip compression, deadbolt, threshold seal
- [ ] Door frame exterior seal at cap joint — where the door frame meets the upper wall panel or cap seam. Inspect with a flashlight. Sealant must be present and continuous. Any gap here is a direct water path into the bedroom wall

## Trash Chute — 260KVS Specific

*The built-in trash chute is a countertop hole that drops directly to an exterior baggage compartment — accessible from outside without opening interior doors.*

- [ ] **Exterior baggage door** — open and close 3 times. Must latch cleanly, weatherstrip must compress fully when closed. This door sees regular use; misalignment or a weak latch is common
- [ ] **Interior countertop cover/cap** — a cover or cap should be fitted over the countertop opening. Verify it is present, seats flush to the countertop surface, and opens/closes cleanly. A missing or loose interior cap is a ventilation issue
- [ ] **No open path for moisture/pests** — with the exterior baggage door closed, there should be no unrestricted path from outside to inside through the chute assembly. Confirm this by looking down the chute from inside with the exterior door shut — you should not see daylight
- [ ] **Clear pathway** — run your hand into the chute from inside. No construction debris, packaging material, or foam insulation blocking the channel

## Split Gray Tanks — 260KVS Specific

*The 260KVS runs two separate 40-gallon gray tanks instead of a single 80-gallon tank. This is normal for this floor plan; just understand how it works.*

- [ ] **Verify both tanks show EMPTY on monitor panel at PDI start** — if either tank is reading partial, the dealer has been using the trailer. Ask why and have them show you a drain
- [ ] **Confirm both tanks drain via same dump valve** (or confirm which valves control which tank if separate) — ask the dealer to walk you through the gray tank dump procedure for this floor plan specifically. Some split-tank units have one shared valve; some have two
- [ ] **Fill water, use sink, verify both tanks register on monitor** — during the water system PDI check, confirm the monitor panel correctly shows both gray tanks increasing as you use water. A non-functional tank sensor on delivery is more common than it should be
- [ ] **Ask about the split** — specifically: which tank fills from which source (kitchen vs. shower/bathroom sink)? Knowing this going in helps you manage boondocking dump timing in the field

## Three-Slide Auto-Leveling — 260KVS Specific

*Three extended slides changes the trailer's load distribution relative to two-slide units. The auto-leveling system needs to be verified with all slides out, on an unlevel surface.*

- [ ] **🔴 Full auto-level cycle with all 3 slides EXTENDED, on an unlevel surface** — do not accept a leveling demo on a flat dealer lot with slides retracted. Insist on:
  - Parking the trailer on a genuinely unlevel surface before the demo
  - Extending all 3 slides fully
  - Running the Ground Control TT full auto-level cycle to completion
  - Trailer must sit level when the cycle ends — confirm with a bubble level inside
- [ ] **No tongue jack overextension** — documented on other Titanium models: auto-level raises the tongue jack to near full extension before completing. Watch the tongue jack throughout the cycle. Any overextension or clunking = demand recalibration before delivery
- [ ] **Battery display accuracy** — documented Titanium-wide: control head reports "batteries low" when solar/charge controller shows full. Have dealer confirm battery state display is accurate vs. actual battery charge
- [ ] **Re-zero procedure in writing** — ask the dealer for the documented Ground Control TT re-zero procedure before you leave. You will likely need it at some point; getting it on delivery day is the right time to get it

## Wheel Bearings — Flag for All Blackstone Buyers

*Over-greasing at factory is a documented Blackstone series-wide issue. Excess grease migrates to brake drums and can cause brake fade or failure.*

- [ ] **Request bearing inspection at PDI** — ask the dealer to pull a wheel and confirm bearing grease level is within spec. Most dealers will do this without friction if asked
- [ ] **Plan a repack within the first 3,000 miles regardless** — even if the PDI inspection passes, the documented pattern is strong enough to justify an early first service
- [ ] **Watch for any brake softness or fade on your first mountain/grade descent** — if you feel anything unusual on a downhill grade, pull over safely and let brakes cool. Do not push through; get them inspected before continuing
