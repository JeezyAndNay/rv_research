## ⚠️ NHTSA Safety Recall — ALL FOUR 2025 MODELS

**Recall Number:** 24V813000
**Affected units:** 2025 model year — 250RKS, 250RDS, 260KRS, 25DVS (plus 20BD, 21MKS, 21RWS, 23BCS, 24KRS, F28RKS — 340 units total)

**Defect:** The cooktop flame may invert when the stove and furnace are operated simultaneously. Inverted flame = injury or fire risk.

**Remedy:** Dealers install a dividing panel between the furnace and cooktop at no charge. Owner notification letters mailed November 5, 2024.

**Action at PDI:** If buying a 2025 unit, confirm recall status is closed — dealer should have completed the panel installation before delivery. Ask for documentation. Do not accept an unrepaired 2025 unit pending a "future service appointment."

**Contact:** Outdoors RV 1-541-962-1866 ext. 222 | NHTSA 1-888-327-4236 | nhtsa.gov

## Floor Plan 1: Blackstone 250RKS (Titanium)

### "Rear Kitchen · Dual Opposing Slides" — The Compact Couples Trailer

The 250RKS is the shorter-wheelbase rear kitchen Blackstone — 31'4" vs the 280RKS at nearly 35 feet. This is the floor plan that gives you the ORV Blackstone experience without committing to a 35-foot trailer. Two opposing slides open up the center living section significantly; the rear kitchen faces the living area so the cook is part of the conversation. Front private bedroom.

### Specs

|---|---|---|

| Exterior length | 31'4" | 31'4" |
| Dry weight | 7,500 lbs | 7,980 lbs |
| GVWR | 11,000 lbs | 11,000 lbs |
| Cargo capacity | 3,500 lbs | 3,020 lbs |
| Slides | 2 | 2 |
| Sleeps | 3 | 3 |
| Fresh water | 80 gal | 80 gal |
| Gray water | 80 gal | 80 gal |
| Black water | 50 gal | 50 gal |
| LPG | 60 lbs (2 tanks) | 60 lbs (2 tanks) |
| Water heater | 10 gal | 10 gal |
| Furnace | 35K BTU | 35K BTU |
| A/C | 15K BTU | 15K BTU |
| Refrigerator | 12V 10 cu ft | 12V 10 cu ft |
| Hitch weight (dry) | 780 lbs | 780 lbs |

### Layout Description

- **Front**: Queen bedroom with wardrobes
- **Mid**: Dual-entry bathroom
- **Center**: Two opposing slides — dinette slide + sofa/recliner slide — opens the main living area
- **Rear**: Kitchen facing forward into the living area. Pantry, 3-burner cooktop, solid surface countertops, overhead cabinets
- **TV**: Swing-out 40" in living area

The "dual opposing" slide configuration is important: both slides open perpendicular to each other on opposite walls, which creates a wider open feel in the center section than a single large slide would. Owners describe the living area as notably spacious for the trailer's footprint.

### What Owners Say

Per [iRV2 — New Outdoors Black Stone 250rks on order](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/new-outdoors-black-stone-250rks-on-order-506465.html): The 250RKS is praised for its suspension quality — tows smoothly and sits higher off the ground than most comparably-sized trailers, which matters on rougher terrain. One owner noted no sway issues running a 12,000 lb equalizer hitch.

Per [iRV2 — 2023 ORV 250RKS Titanium Boondocking Power Questions](https://www.irv2.com/threads/2023-orv-250rks-titanium-boondocking-power-questions.2199381/): Boondocking discussion reveals that the stock 200Ah AGM battery bank is marginal for the 12V compressor fridge during extended off-grid stays. Community consensus: real-world capable boondocking requires 280–320Ah lithium minimum with 400W+ solar. Owners who've upgraded report the platform handles off-grid very well after the battery upgrade.

Per [RV Insider — 250RKS Reviews](https://www.rvinsider.com/Outdoors-Rv-Blackstone-Titanium-250RKS-Travel-Trailers-RV-Reviews): 5-star owner in Oregon noted "thermal shielding is fantastic — in 100 degree heat outside, base inside was 82" and praised the dinette seating for 4. A 4.4-star reviewer (Texas) praised the suspension and exterior build but called out defective auto-leveling electronics reporting wrong battery status despite solar showing full, and rated interior appliance quality as "inexpensive."

**Manufacturer support:** Consistently reported as strong — Outdoors RV answers the phone, ships parts promptly, and walks owners through installation patiently. Multiple owners note this as a distinguishing factor vs. Indiana manufacturers.

### Known Issues (Model-Specific)

| # | Issue | Source | PDI Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 1 | **Black tank flush plumbed backwards** — documented on 250RKS units, same defect as 28DBS | iRV2 owner report | Run tank flush, verify water flows INTO tank (not out) |
| 🔴 2 | **Underbelly coroplast sagging + missing screws** — panels not properly secured at factory | iRV2 2023 250RKS owner | Inspect underbelly before leaving lot; photograph any gaps or sags |
| 🔴 3 | **Water pump vulnerable to freezing** — pump is located in the left rear corner; at extreme cold (5°F + high winds) the pump intake line between pump and tank can freeze despite "heated underbelly" claim | iRV2 thread | Verify pump location at delivery; ask about winterization procedure for this specific pump location |
| 🔴 4 | **Schwintek wardrobe slide failure** — one documented case of Schwintek slide requiring full rebuild within 2 years, just past the 1-year warranty | iRV2 | Run wardrobe slide 3 full cycles at PDI; listen for motor strain |
| 🟡 5 | **Propane hose braces missing screws** — gas hose braces to stove not fully secured at factory | iRV2 2023 250RKS inspection | Inspect propane line routing and verify all brace screws present |
| 🟡 6 | **Slam hatch latch installed incorrectly** — passenger side slam hatch latch misaligned at factory | iRV2 2023 250RKS inspection | Test all exterior hatch latches |
| 🟡 7 | **Auto-leveling false battery warning** — Titanium models report "batteries low" while solar controller shows full charge. Needs re-zeroing of the control head | iRV2 / RV Insider | Have dealer demonstrate full level cycle; get re-zero procedure in writing |
| 🟡 8 | **Interior appliances: budget quality** — multiple reviewers flag that interior electronics and appliances don't match the exterior build quality at the price point | RV Insider | No PDI fix, but set expectations accordingly |
| 🔴 9 | **RECALL 24V813000** — 2025 units: cooktop flame inversion. Must be repaired before delivery | NHTSA | Confirm recall closed on all 2025 units |

### Best For
Couples who want the ORV Blackstone build quality in a sub-32-foot package. The dual opposing slides create more usable interior space per foot of trailer than many competing floor plans. The rear kitchen layout works well for camp cooking. Weaker off-grid out of the box than the BackCountry 25DVS (200W solar vs 400W, no generator), but the platform can be upgraded.

## Floor Plan 2: Blackstone 250RDS (Titanium)

### "Rear Dinette · Front Private Bedroom" — The Couple's View Trailer

The 250RDS puts the mountain dinette at the rear with large windows facing the campsite — you eat while looking out the back. The kitchen is mid-trailer. Private front bedroom with a dedicated wardrobe slide gives the bedroom more closet space than the 250RKS. Sleeps 5 with the sleeper sofa and convertible dinette. The extra foot of exterior length over the 250RKS (32'4" vs 31'4") comes from the wardrobe slide configuration.

### Layout Description

- **Front**: Queen bedroom. Wardrobe slide on the bedroom wall adds significant closet space — one of the larger bedroom storage configurations in this class
- **Mid-front**: Full bathroom
- **Mid**: Kitchen with flip-up counter for extended workspace. Sunken range. Solid surface countertops
- **Center**: Thomas Payne sleeper sofa in the living area
- **Rear**: Mountain dinette with large windows facing the campsite — the defining feature of this floor plan. Dinette converts for additional sleeping

The RDS layout is specifically designed so the social space (dinette) faces outward. At a dispersed site, you're eating with a view of your actual camping location rather than facing the interior of the trailer.

### What Owners Say

Per [iRV2 — New 250RDS Blackstone Titanium Owner with questions](https://www.irv2.com/threads/new-250rds-blackstone-titanium-owner-with-questions.1836890/): New owner reported loving the trailer after a 10-day trip to British Columbia — fireplace kept the living room cozy, furnace performed well, and the two-person camping setup felt well-suited to the floor plan.

Per [iRV2 — Question for any 25RDS owners](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/question-for-any-25rds-owners-634446.html): Active owner community with generally positive long-term reports. Most reported issues are minor and addressed early in ownership.

**Manufacturer support:** Same pattern as all ORV models — consistently reported as responsive, ships parts, walks through repairs by phone.

### Known Issues (Model-Specific)

| # | Issue | Source | PDI Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 1 | **Drafty bedroom closets** — documented pattern on 2024 250RDS units: cold air infiltrates through the tops of the bedroom closets. In cold weather camping, this is a significant four-season problem for a trailer sold on four-season capability | iRV2 thread "2024 250RDS bedroom closets are drafty" | Open closet doors, run your hand along the top seal — feel for airflow. In cold temps, any draft here is a defect to document |
| 🔴 2 | **Mattress freezes to front wall** — in cold conditions, condensation at the front bedroom wall can freeze the mattress to the wall. Related to the air infiltration at the front of the sleeping area | iRV2 owner report | Owner fix: silicone + duct tape at mattress-to-wall gap. Check the front wall gap at delivery; request factory sealing if any gap exists |
| 🟡 3 | **Auto-leveling tongue jack overextension** — documented on this model: auto-level cycle raised the tongue jack too high, producing a loud clacking/grinding noise. Needs immediate re-zeroing of the Lippert Ground Control TT control head | iRV2 Lippert Auto Leveling System thread | Run full auto-level cycle at PDI. If tongue jack goes to full extension before system levels, stop immediately and demand dealer re-zero before delivery |
| 🟡 4 | **Floor support contact** — plywood support blocks under the floor don't make full contact with the subfloor in some units, leaving slight movement during use (noticed while showering). Not structural but indicates inconsistent quality control in the floor assembly | iRV2 owner report | Press floor in bathroom and near dinette — should feel completely solid |
| 🔴 5 | **RECALL 24V813000** — 2025 units: cooktop flame inversion. Must be repaired before delivery | NHTSA | Confirm recall closed on all 2025 units |

### Best For
Couples who prioritize the campsite view while eating and socializing. The rear dinette with panoramic windows is genuinely differentiated from any rear kitchen layout. The wardrobe slide gives the bedroom more functional storage than most trailers at this length. The 250RDS is also the lightest of the four new Blackstone floor plans (7,300 lbs Mountain), which matters for fuel economy on long tow days.

## Floor Plan 3: Blackstone 260KRS (Titanium)

### "Rear Kitchen · Panoramic Window · Front Bedroom" — The Longer Kitchen Trailer

The 260KRS is a foot longer than the 250RKS but uses that extra foot entirely in the kitchen and living section rather than the bedroom. The defining feature is the large panoramic window across the entire rear wall behind the kitchen — there are no overhead cabinets at the rear because the window takes that wall space. This creates a uniquely open, light-filled kitchen environment that's unlike the more enclosed rear kitchens in most trailers. The trash chute is a practical detail: a hole in the countertop drops trash directly into the outdoor baggage compartment.

- **Awning**: 20' single awning

The 260KRS was updated to the 260KVS (3 slides vs 2) for newer model years. If you see a 260KVS on the lot, it's the evolution of this floor plan with an added slide; the KRS with 2 slides is mechanically simpler.

### What Owners Say

The 260KRS is a newer floor plan with thinner owner community data than the 250 series. No dedicated long-form owner reviews found on iRV2 or RV Insider at time of research. The closest data points:

Per [iRV2 — "Info needed on Blackstone Titanium 260 RLS"](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/info-needed-on-blackstone-titanium-260-rls-392303.html): while focused on the RLS, this thread reveals the 260-length Blackstone is well-regarded for suspension quality — the MorRyde AllTrek handles the longer wheelbase cleanly. Owners of similar 260-class ORV trailers report the kitchen layout works well for road trips where campsite cooking matters.

Per [iRV2 — "ORV build quality"](https://www.irv2.com/threads/orv-build-quality.2028281/page-4): One critical review describes a unit where "insulation was not cut to fit, roof structure not secured to wall plates, and panel glue not secure." This is not a 260KRS-specific finding but indicates QC variance is real across ORV production — make the PDI count.

General Blackstone owner community sentiment: The rear kitchen Blackstone layout with ORV suspension performs better over rough roads than most rear kitchen trailers because the suspension dampens the movement that causes pantry items to slide and break. One 280RKSB owner confirmed no breakage issues over 2 years, specifically crediting the suspension.

**⚠️ Note:** The 260KRS is being phased alongside the 260KVS (3-slide version). If considering the KRS, verify parts availability and dealer familiarity with this specific floor plan before buying.

## Floor Plan 4: BackCountry 25DVS (Titanium)

### "Dual Vanity Suite · Off-Grid Architecture · Generator Standard" — The Serious Boondocker

The 25DVS is ORV's response to couples who want genuine off-grid capability in the BackCountry platform without buying a bunkhouse. "DVS" = Dual Vanity Suite — the bathroom has dual sinks/vanities, a feature uncommon in a 26-foot travel trailer and significant for a couple sharing a trailer for extended off-grid stays. The rear has a large panoramic window configuration with a convertible dinette; the front has the private bedroom. One slide.

The BackCountry platform is architecturally different from the Blackstone: it includes an onboard Cummins Onan 3.6K LP generator as standard equipment, 400W expandable solar (vs 200W on Blackstone), 100-gallon fresh water tank (vs 80 on Blackstone), and a custom cambered HD 8" off-road chassis specifically designed for backcountry terrain.

### Layout Description

- **Front**: Queen private bedroom
- **Mid**: Dual vanity bathroom — two sinks, full bath. This is the DVS differentiator; couples sharing a trailer for 7–14 day off-grid stays will feel this every morning
- **Center**: Thomas Payne Trifold sleeper sofa in the living area
- **Rear**: Extra-large panoramic window. Dinette that converts to a large bed. Generator compartment integrated into the exterior
- **Slide**: Living/dinette area slide — opens up the main living space when set up

**BackCountry vs Blackstone architecture difference:**
The 25DVS has 20 more gallons of fresh water (100 vs 80), 400W vs 200W solar, a standard Onan generator, and a purpose-built off-road chassis. For serious dispersed camping this is not a cosmetic difference — it's a different class of off-grid capability. Trade-off: only 1 slide means the interior is tighter when set up than the 2-slide Blackstone models.

### What Owners Say

The 25DVS is a relatively new floor plan (introduced ~2023) with a thinner owner community than the established Blackstone models. Direct long-term reviews are limited but contextual data is available.

Per [iRV2 — Back Country 25DVS Blue OX WDH](https://www.irv2.com/threads/back-country-25dvs-blue-ox-wdh.2073171/): Owner discussion confirms the 25DVS is the same basic floor plan architecture as the 25RDS but in the BackCountry platform (with generator compartment, off-road chassis). The thread covers hitch setup — owners confirm the 25DVS's 950-lb dry hitch weight and ~8,000–8,500 lb loaded weight benefit from a weight distribution hitch even behind a 3/4-ton or 1-ton truck for improved stability and ride quality on rough terrain. Blue Ox Sway Pro or Equalizer are the community recommendations.

Per [RV Insider — BackCountry reviews](https://www.rvinsider.com/Outdoors-Rv-Back-Country-RV-Reviews?make=Outdoors+RV&model=Back+Country): BackCountry 20BD 2021 owner (3 months): "Great floor plan, tows well" — but documented at delivery: defective drawer, generator regulator flow issue, backwards water tank heater wiring, unsecured water pump, broken window blind, roof bubble, loose sawdust in cabinets. Despite this list, the owner remained positive, attributing issues to typical factory QC lapses rather than systemic build failures. ORV warranty support resolved items.

Per [iRV2 — Any Outdoors BD Back Country Owners out there?](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/any-outdoors-bd-back-country-owners-out-there-514930.html): Consistent praise from BackCountry owners for the off-road performance and build quality of the platform; the generator and solar setup genuinely support multi-day boondocking without anxiety.

**Generator note from 22RWS owner (iRV2):** Rough-running generator traced to improper spark plug gap (.028" vs spec .020") combined with fuel delivery and propane hose leak issues. This was documented on a 2022 BackCountry RWS, not the 25DVS specifically — but the generator is the same Onan 3.6K across the BackCountry line, and this failure mode is documented.

### Known Issues (Model-Specific + BackCountry Platform)

| # | Issue | Source | PDI Test |
|---|---|---|---|

| 🟡 6 | **Roof bubble** — documented on BackCountry 20BD: delamination bubble in roof material | RV Insider 2021 BackCountry review | Walk the roof, press every inch — especially around A/C base and vent penetrations. Any soft spot or lift is a defect |

The 1-slide design is mechanically simpler but you feel it on rainy days inside | Design tradeoff | Walk through with all occupants before committing to this floor plan |

### Best For
Couples doing serious dispersed and off-grid camping who want genuine extended-stay capability without compromise. The 100-gal fresh tank, 400W solar, Onan generator, and purpose-built off-road chassis make this the most capable off-grid platform of the four new floor plans — and arguably the most capable couples' off-grid trailer in the ORV lineup. The dual vanity bathroom is a meaningful quality-of-life feature for extended stays. Trade-off vs. the Blackstone models: you get one slide and a tighter interior in exchange for genuinely better off-grid infrastructure.
