# Outdoors RV — PDI Additions: New Floor Plans
*Blackstone 250RKS · Blackstone 250RDS · Blackstone 260KRS · BackCountry 25DVS*
*Research date: 2026-06-17*

> **How to use this file:** These are the model-specific PDI additions for the four new floor plans. The existing `Outdoors RV/02_pdi_checklist.md` in the repo covers all generic ORV checks (auto leveling, water system, stabilizer jacks, generator, slides, roof seals, tires, documentation) — run those first. This file adds the items specific to each new floor plan.

## ⚠️ Before You Even Schedule the PDI — All Four 2025 Models

**NHTSA Recall 24V813000 must be closed before delivery.**

All four 2025 models (250RKS, 250RDS, 260KRS, 25DVS) are covered by this recall:
- **Defect:** Cooktop flame inverts when stove and furnace operate simultaneously — fire/injury risk
- **Remedy:** Dealer installs a dividing panel between furnace and cooktop at no charge
- **Owner notification:** Mailed November 5, 2024

**What to do:**
- [ ] Before scheduling your PDI, call the dealer and ask: "Has recall 24V813000 been completed on this unit?"
- [ ] Ask them to email you the closed recall documentation before your arrival
- [ ] If they say "we'll do it at delivery" — that is not acceptable. The recall must be completed and verified before you sign
- [ ] If they say it doesn't apply to your unit, ask them to pull the VIN through NHTSA. Verify at nhtsa.gov yourself

## Blackstone 250RKS — Model-Specific PDI Items

*Run these AFTER completing the full generic ORV PDI checklist.*

### Water System — 250RKS Specific

- [ ] **🔴 Black tank flush direction** — documented defect on this model (same as 28DBS): flush valve plumbed backwards at factory. Test: connect city water, open black tank flush valve, confirm water flows INTO the tank through the flush port. If flushing causes tank level to drop or water comes out where it shouldn't, the valve is backwards. Do not accept the trailer without correction
- [ ] **Inspect underbelly coroplast** — walk the full underbelly and look for sagging panels or visible missing screws/washers. Photographed 2023 250RKS units showed loose panels at multiple points. Documented before signing; require dealer to resecure before delivery
- [ ] **Verify water pump location and cold weather vulnerability** — locate the water pump (left rear corner area). Discuss winterization procedure for this specific pump placement. At extreme cold (5°F or below with wind), the pump intake line is vulnerable to freezing even with the heated underbelly. Know this going in

### Propane System — 250RKS Specific

- [ ] **🔴 Propane hose braces to stove** — documented on 2023 250RKS: screws missing from propane hose braces at the range. Inspect all propane line routing and verify every brace/clamp has its fasteners. Pull gently on the line to confirm it's secure
- [ ] Check propane regulator connections while you're there — document any loose fitting or incorrect sizing (documented across BackCountry line; verify on Blackstone too)

### Latches and Exterior — 250RKS Specific

- [ ] **Slam hatch latch alignment** — documented on 2023 250RKS: passenger-side slam hatch latch installed off-center/misaligned. Test every exterior hatch: open fully, release, confirm it latches cleanly without requiring force or repositioning
- [ ] Inspect all exterior baggage door latches in the same way

### Slides — 250RKS Specific

- [ ] **Schwintek wardrobe slide** — documented failure on 250RKS within 2 years. At PDI: run the wardrobe/closet slide through 3 full in/out cycles. Listen for motor strain, one-sided walking, or grinding. Any hesitation or noise is a reason to demand inspection of the Schwintek motors and rails before signing
- [ ] Run all slides (both, 3 full cycles each) — standard checklist item but critical here given documented Schwintek history

### Electronics — 250RKS Specific

- [ ] **Auto-leveling battery false alarm** — documented on Titanium models: control head reports "batteries low" while solar controller shows full charge. Have dealer demonstrate a full auto-level cycle from unlevel position AND confirm battery state display agrees with solar state display. Get the re-zero procedure in writing — you will likely need it

## Blackstone 250RDS — Model-Specific PDI Items

*Run these AFTER completing the full generic ORV PDI checklist.*

### Bedroom Air Infiltration — 250RDS Specific (Critical in Cold Climates)

- [ ] **🔴 Bedroom closet cold air infiltration** — documented pattern on 2024 250RDS: cold air enters through the tops of the bedroom wardrobe closets. In a trailer sold for four-season mountain camping, this is a significant defect.
  - **How to test at PDI:** If possible, schedule your PDI on a cold day. With the trailer doors closed, open the closet doors and hold your hand along the top seal/gap. Feel for any airflow. Even a slight draft here is not acceptable.
  - **If warm-day PDI:** Ask the dealer specifically about this documented issue. Request they inspect the closet top seals. Require written documentation that the seals are intact
  - **Owner fix (post-purchase):** Silicone sealant at closet top gaps; some owners added foam tape weatherstripping
- [ ] **Front bedroom wall gap at mattress** — documented: a gap between the mattress head and the front bedroom wall allows cold air infiltration. In freezing temps, condensation can freeze the mattress to the wall.
  - Check the front wall gap at delivery — run your hand along the mattress-to-wall gap
  - If any gap exists larger than 1/4", request dealer sealing before delivery

### Auto-Leveling — 250RDS Specific

- [ ] **🔴 Tongue jack overextension** — documented on 250RDS: the auto-level cycle raised the tongue jack to full extension, producing a loud clacking/grinding noise before stopping. This is a control head calibration issue that needs re-zeroing.
  - At PDI: watch the tongue jack throughout the full auto-level cycle. If it extends toward full height without the system completing, stop the cycle manually and demand dealer recalibration before delivery
  - Do not leave without the re-zero procedure in writing and a successful demo of a full clean level cycle

### Floor Structure — 250RDS Specific

- [ ] **Floor support contact** — documented: plywood sub-floor support blocks in some units don't make full contact with the floor assembly, leaving minor flex during use.
  - Test: stand in the bathroom, rock side to side, then bounce lightly. Stand at the dinette, apply pressure. The floor should feel completely solid — zero flex
  - Any flex, especially near the bathroom, is worth documenting and requesting correction

## Blackstone 260KRS — Model-Specific PDI Items

*Run these AFTER completing the full generic ORV PDI checklist.*

### Rear Kitchen — 260KRS Specific

- [ ] **Panoramic rear window seal** — the 260KRS's defining feature (the large rear window with no overhead cabinets) means the entire rear wall seal is a single large window unit. Inspect every edge of this window for complete sealing — exterior lap sealant at every corner and edge. Any void here becomes a water intrusion point directly into the rear kitchen
- [ ] **No overhead cabinet = more window to seal** — unlike other rear kitchen trailers, there's no cabinet covering the upper rear wall. This means more sealant perimeter to inspect
- [ ] **Trash chute function** — the built-in trash chute (countertop hole to exterior baggage compartment) should have a cover or cap on both ends. Open the exterior baggage door, confirm the chute is clear and the exterior access seals cleanly against weather
- [ ] **Under-counter microwave** — microwave is located below the countertop rather than overhead. Verify it slides out (or opens) correctly and the trim is properly fitted

### Cargo — 260KRS Specific

- [ ] **Cargo capacity watch** — at 3,200 lbs Mountain / 2,720 lbs Titanium, the 260KRS has the tightest cargo capacity of the four new models. Verify your loaded weight plan: fresh water (80 gal = 668 lbs), food, gear, people. Run the numbers before you arrive so you know your ceiling

## BackCountry 25DVS — Model-Specific PDI Items

*Run these AFTER completing the full generic ORV PDI checklist. The 25DVS has a generator standard — run the full generator section of the generic checklist.*

### Recall — 25DVS Specific (Critical)

- [ ] **🔴 RECALL 24V813000** — this recall is particularly important on the 25DVS because the BackCountry platform has both a cooktop AND a furnace operating in a tighter space than a Blackstone. The risk of simultaneous operation is higher in a compact floor plan. Confirm dividing panel is installed and documented before delivery

### Generator — 25DVS Specific (Generator is Standard, Not Optional)

The 25DVS includes the Cummins Onan 3.6K LP generator as standard. The full generator PDI section from the existing checklist applies here. Additional items:

- [ ] **🔴 Spark plug gap** — documented across BackCountry line: Onan 3.6K shipped with .028" gap instead of spec .020". Pull one plug at PDI, verify gap with feeler gauge. If wrong, require correction before delivery
- [ ] **🔴 Propane hose at generator** — documented propane hose leak at generator connection on BackCountry 22RWS. With generator OFF, apply soapy water solution (or electronic leak detector) to all propane connections at the generator — regulator, supply line, connection fitting. Zero bubbles required before accepting
- [ ] Run generator loaded: start it, turn on A/C while running. Verify the generator handles the load without sagging or shutting down. Smooth, steady operation at full load = good
- [ ] Log generator hour meter at delivery

### Plumbing — 25DVS Specific

- [ ] **🔴 Water tank heater wiring** — documented on BackCountry 20BD: tank heater wired backwards at factory. Test: turn on tank heater, wait 10 minutes, touch the heater element area or monitor tank temp. It should be getting warm. Cold = backwards wiring
- [ ] **🔴 Water pump mounting** — documented on BackCountry 20BD: water pump not secured to its mount. Locate the pump, push and rock it with your hand — should be completely solid with no movement
- [ ] Connect city water slowly, walk every fitting — water fill line clamp documentation from the generic checklist applies here; BackCountry line has documented wrong-size clamps

### Roof and Structure — 25DVS Specific

- [ ] **Roof delamination bubble** — documented on BackCountry 20BD: bubble in roof material from delamination. Walk the full roof carefully, press every section. Any soft spot or lift that springs back = delamination. Photograph everything
- [ ] Check all roof vent bases and A/C mount perimeter — standard checklist, but the BackCountry platform has documented roof QC issues

### Interior — 25DVS Specific

- [ ] **Cabinet debris** — documented on BackCountry 20BD: loose sawdust and manufacturing debris in cabinets. Open and inspect every cabinet interior before accepting the trailer
- [ ] **Dual vanity bathroom** — test both sinks: fill and drain each independently, verify both faucets have correct hot/cold orientation, check under both vanities for any loose plumbing connections
- [ ] **Convertible rear dinette** — convert to sleeping position and back. Should convert smoothly; cushion alignment should be clean in both positions
- [ ] **Thomas Payne sleeper sofa** — open to sleep position and back. Check all mechanism points

### Solar System — 25DVS Specific

- [ ] **400W solar** — verify solar controller display is functional and reading correctly. With any daylight, solar should show nonzero input watts. Verify battery state matches solar controller reading (the auto-leveling "batteries low" false alarm is documented across Titanium models)
- [ ] Ask dealer: has the solar system been configured and tested? Expandable = it can accept more panels. Confirm the 400W base is wired and operational before adding more
