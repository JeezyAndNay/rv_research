# Grand Design Imagine — PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*2970RL · 2810BH · 2800BH*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

**Time budget: 3–4 hours.** Grand Design dealers generally respond well to informed buyers — the brand has cultivated a reputation around owner engagement and the MyGrandRV.com community is well-known. Come prepared and most service techs will meet you there.

---

## The Day Before — Call the Dealer

> *"I'm coming in tomorrow for my PDI on the Imagine [floor plan]. Before I arrive, can you make sure the unit is connected to shore power, city water is connected, and the refrigerator has been running for at least four hours? I'll need about three to four hours for the inspection."*

If they push back on time:

> *"I've done a lot of research on the Imagine line and I have a documented checklist. Three to four hours is what I'll need. If that's not possible tomorrow, let's find a day that works."*

---

## Arrival — Set the Frame (5 minutes)

When you meet the service tech or sales rep:

> *"I appreciate the time today. I've been researching Grand Design specifically — I know the brand builds a solid product and I'm excited about this trailer. I just want to go through everything systematically so we both know the unit is right before I sign. I have a checklist and I'd like to follow it. Fair?"*

Immediately follow with:

> *"Can I get a copy of the PDI form your team completed? And at the end, I'll need a signed copy with anything we find today added to it."*

---

## Bunk Room Door — START HERE (2810BH / 2800BH only, 5 minutes)

The bunk room sliding door is the #1 documented defect on these floor plans. Start here so you know what you're dealing with before you invest four hours.

> *"Let's start with the bunk room door. I've read about a known issue with the floor guide stops on bunkhouse Imagine models — I want to see it move."*

Open and close the bunk door slowly, then quickly, three full cycles:

> *"Can you push it laterally while it's on the track? I want to see that it stays seated."*

If the door pops off track or binds:

> *"This is the documented floor guide stop issue. I'd like this repaired and retested before we continue. Can the service department look at it now?"*

---

## Dump Valves — Identify Before They're Mixed Up (10 minutes)

> *"I want to verify the gray and black tank dump valve labels before we go any further. There's a documented case of these being swapped at the factory on recent model years."*

> *"Can you run water through the black tank flush and then we'll pull each valve to see which tank actually drains?"*

Watch: pull the valve labeled "black" — water/sludge from the black tank should drain. Pull "gray" — gray water should drain. If labels are swapped:

> *"The labels are swapped — I'd like that corrected in writing on the PDI form before I take the unit. I don't want to be at a campground pulling the wrong handle."*

---

## Refrigerator — Verify Cold Before Anything Else (5 minutes)

> *"What temperature is the fridge reading right now? It should have been running at least four hours."*

Bring a small thermometer and check yourself:

> *"I have a thermometer — let me verify the temp inside."*

Must be below 40°F. If it's at or near ambient:

> *"A refrigerator that's been on four hours and isn't cooling is a documented issue on the Imagine line. I'd like the service department to look at the burner assembly before I go any further. Can that happen today?"*

Do not accept a non-cooling refrigerator with a promise to "look at it later" or "it just needs more time."

---

## Roof — Get Up There (15–20 minutes)

> *"I want to walk the roof. Can we get a ladder?"*

Walk the full roof perimeter and press every sealant joint:

> *"I'm going to press along all four corners of the roof and the rear wall seam. I've read about membrane adhesion issues on the Imagine — I want to see it before I drive it."*

Press along the rear wall-to-roof transition (most critical on 2970RL):

> *"This rear seam is where I've seen the most membrane lift reported. I'm pressing the full length."*

While on the roof:

> *"I'm also going to photograph every sealant joint up here with timestamps. This is my warranty baseline."*

If any membrane is lifting:

> *"The membrane is lifting here. I'd like the service department to re-adhere and seal this before I take delivery. Can you write that on the PDI form?"*

---

## Water System — Full Pressurized Walk (20 minutes)

> *"Is city water connected? I want to do a complete pressurized walk before we move on."*

Turn on the water and walk the entire trailer:

> *"I want to open every faucet — kitchen, both bathroom sinks, shower — and look underneath every cabinet while the water is on."*

Check under every sink while running:

> *"I'm also looking at the floor under each cabinet. Any moisture or water staining is a flag for prior leaks."*

Then:

> *"Let's test the GFCI circuit. I've read that all outlets on some Imagine models are daisy-chained to a single GFCI — I want to verify that running two appliances simultaneously doesn't trip the circuit."*

Plug in the microwave and ask the tech to plug in a coffee maker into a different outlet and run both:

> *"Both running, no trip — good. Now let me trip the GFCI and see which outlets go dead."*

After reset, confirm everything restores.

---

## Slides — Extend and Inspect (10 minutes)

> *"Let's run all the slides out."*

Watch each slide extend fully. Then:

> *"I'm going to walk the perimeter of each slide seal and press every inch of the rubber wiper seal. I'm looking for tears, gaps, or separation."*

Retract all slides. If any hesitation:

> *"That hesitation on the retract could be a Schwintek track alignment issue. Can your service tech take a look at the rail before I sign?"*

---

## Bunk Room and Interior Walk (15 minutes)

> *"Let's walk the interior with everything open."*

Push on every wall-mounted item:

> *"I want to push on every shelf, bracket, and towel bar. I'm checking whether they're anchored into studs or just into the wall panel."*

Run your palm along countertops:

> *"I've read about countertop warping on the Imagine — I want to feel each surface for bubbling."*

Open every cabinet and drawer:

> *"I'm checking that every drawer slides without binding and every cabinet closes flush."*

On the bunks (2800BH / 2810BH):

> *"I'm going to sit on each bunk and rock it. I want to know the ladder is solid and the mattress supports are holding."*

---

## Appliances — Full Run (15 minutes)

> *"Let's run everything. I want to see the A/C units, furnace, cooktop, oven, microwave, and water heater."*

- A/C units: run each one 5 minutes, feel for cold air at every vent
- Furnace: full heat cycle, confirm warm air at all registers
- Cooktop: light every burner, confirm clean ignition
- Oven: set to 350°F, verify it holds temperature
- Microwave: heat water 60 seconds
- Water heater: run a full cycle

If anything doesn't perform:

> *"[Appliance] isn't functioning. I want this repaired or replaced before I take delivery — can you write that on the PDI form with a specific date for resolution?"*

---

## Final Questions Before Signing

> *"What's your average turnaround time on warranty parts from Grand Design? I want to know what to expect if I have a claim in my first year."*

> *"Is there a technician on staff who specifically works on Grand Design trailers regularly, or do you service all brands?"*

> *"If I have a warranty issue and you can't source the part quickly, does Grand Design authorize me to go to another dealer or handle it myself with reimbursement?"*

The answers to these tell you what your ownership experience looks like before you commit.

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up

> *"Here's my list of items for the PDI form."*

State everything you found. Then:

> *"For anything that can be fixed today, I'd like it fixed and retested before I sign. For anything that needs parts or scheduling — bunk door guide stops, dump valve labels, roof membrane re-adhesion — I want a specific date written on the PDI form. I'll come back for those."*

If the dealer pushes "we'll take care of it under warranty after you drive home":

> *"I'd rather not take delivery until these items are resolved. Once I sign and leave the lot, these become warranty claims. Before I sign, they're your cost to fix at no approval process needed. Let's get them right now."*

---

## Final Checklist Before Keys Change Hands

- [ ] Signed PDI form with all findings documented and both parties' signatures
- [ ] Bunk room door verified on track — not jumping (2800BH/2810BH)
- [ ] Dump valve labels verified correct and noted on PDI form
- [ ] Refrigerator confirmed below 40°F with your thermometer
- [ ] Roof membrane pressed and no lifting at corners or rear seam
- [ ] Water system pressure-walked with no leaks
- [ ] GFCI circuit verified — dual appliance test passed
- [ ] All slides extended and retracted with no errors
- [ ] All appliances tested and functioning
- [ ] All "fix before delivery" items resolved OR scheduled in writing with date
- [ ] Dated photos of all exterior seals
- [ ] Full warranty documentation in hand (1-yr base, 3-yr structural, 5-yr frame)
- [ ] Service department direct contact: name, phone, email
- [ ] Registered with Grand Design on delivery day
