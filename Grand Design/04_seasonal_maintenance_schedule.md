# Grand Design Imagine — Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*2970RL · 2810BH · 2800BH*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

---

## What Makes Imagine Maintenance Specific

The Imagine uses standard Elkhart construction — which means the maintenance calendar looks like most travel trailers, but with a few specifics:

- **One-piece TPO rubber roof** — the most critical annual maintenance item. TPO sealant degrades with UV exposure and must be inspected and re-applied every year. Grand Design's water damage warranty exclusion follows the same "lack of maintenance" language as Alliance and others.
- **OSB subfloor** — more moisture-sensitive than Azdel or plywood. Any roof or sidewall leak that reaches the subfloor is expensive to fix. Sealant maintenance is not optional.
- **Schwintek in-wall slides** — require track cleaning and periodic lubrication to prevent motor overload errors.
- **Rubber slide seals** — UV degrades them; 303 Aerospace Protectant twice per season.
- **Standard Dexter axles** — inspect annually; bent spindle issues have been documented on some 2021–2023 units, worth keeping an eye on wheel bearing condition.

---

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four exterior sides — any new seal gaps, cracks, or delamination since last trip?
- [ ] Check all compartment door latches — close and latch each one
- [ ] Confirm propane tanks are full and regulator hose shows no cracks
- [ ] Run all slides out and back in once — smooth operation before leaving
- [ ] Lights check: running lights, brake lights, turn signals with tow vehicle connected
- [ ] Verify breakaway cable is connected to tow vehicle
- [ ] Tire pressure cold — verify against spec for loaded weight
- [ ] Safety chains attached correctly

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk the exterior — note any new roof seal concerns, corner cap damage, or road debris impact
- [ ] Clean debris from slide rubber wiper seals before it sits (dirt and grit accelerate seal wear)
- [ ] Log any interior issues while fresh — rattles, leaks, anything that didn't work right
- [ ] If unit sat in rain: check interior for any wet spots at ceiling, windows, or slideout floor

---

## Spring / Season Open (3–4 hours)
*Most important maintenance event of the year*

### Roof — Annual Inspection and Re-Sealing

The TPO rubber roof is the most important maintenance item on any Imagine. UV degrades the lap sealant faster than the roof membrane itself.

- [ ] **Get on the roof** — use a 6-foot ladder at the front cap or ladder on rear bumper if equipped
- [ ] Walk the full roof — no running; step carefully; one-piece TPO can be walked but respect it
- [ ] Inspect every sealant joint: A/C bases, vent rims, MaxxAir fan rings, solar brackets (if equipped), antenna, all four roof-to-wall perimeter edges
- [ ] Press each sealant bead with your fingernail — it should be pliable and intact. Cracking, crumbling, or pulling away means re-seal now.
- [ ] **Pay special attention to the four corners** — most common TPO lift location
- [ ] **2970RL:** inspect the full rear wall-to-roof seam — this is the documented high-risk spot
- [ ] Clean any compromised area with a dry cloth and re-apply Dicor 501LSW (horizontal) or 505LSW (vertical)
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint from the roof, date-stamped** — warranty baseline and resale documentation

### Sidewalls and Seams

- [ ] Walk all four sides — press along every vertical seam, corner cap, and window frame
- [ ] Any soft spots in the sidewall panel = moisture in the laminate — investigate immediately
- [ ] Re-seal any gaps or cracking with 505LSW non-sag
- [ ] Check the front cap seam where fiberglass meets the aluminum sidewall — common separation point on any trailer

### Slide Seals

- [ ] Extend all slides fully
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals on all four sides of every slide — no tears, no gaps, no separation
- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace Protectant to all rubber slide seals — prevents UV cracking
- [ ] Inspect slide membrane (top seal) — any lifting or tearing needs attention before the season
- [ ] Clean slide tracks with a dry cloth — remove any grit or debris
- [ ] Apply Camco Slide-Out Dry Lube (silicone-based) to all slide tracks — do not use WD-40 or petroleum-based lubricant on Schwintek tracks
- [ ] Retract slides and verify smooth operation

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines — open every faucet until water runs clear (pink antifreeze) or until no air burps (blow-out method)
- [ ] Reconnect and install new water filter cartridge
- [ ] Pressurize city water — walk entire trailer for 30 minutes under pressure, checking all lines and under all sinks
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank
- [ ] **Water heater bypass: return bypass valve to normal position before running hot water**
- [ ] Flush water heater tank with fresh water before use

### Appliances — Annual Start-Up

- [ ] Both A/C units: run 20 minutes, verify cold air at all vents — clean evaporator coil filter (typically a foam insert behind the ceiling return)
- [ ] Furnace: run a full heat cycle — **check cooktop burner orifices for spider nests** (spider webs in orifices cause yellow flames and incomplete combustion; any winter storage creates risk)
- [ ] Light all cooktop burners and verify clean blue flame
- [ ] Water heater: full run cycle, both electric and propane modes
- [ ] Refrigerator: 24 hours on electric, verify below 40°F

### Chassis and Tires

- [ ] Check tire sidewalls for cracking and check date codes — replace any tire over 5–6 years regardless of tread
- [ ] Tire pressure cold — verify against spec for your loaded weight
- [ ] Inspect lug nuts — torque to spec (typically 90–100 ft-lbs for Dexter axles — verify your specific spec)
- [ ] Check wheel bearing condition: spin each wheel by hand, feel for roughness or heat discoloration on the hub
- [ ] Grease packed bearings annually; inspect sealed bearings for heat marks
- [ ] Inspect brakes through wheel opening: look for worn pads or corrosion on brake magnets

---

## Mid-Summer Check (1–2 hours)

- [ ] Roof spot-check — focus on any seals that were borderline at spring open; summer UV heat accelerates degradation
- [ ] Re-condition slide rubber seals — second 303 Aerospace application, especially if camping in sun/heat
- [ ] Check all exterior seals after any long road trip — road vibration can open gaps in lap sealant
- [ ] Check any bunk door or slide that was marginal at PDI — monitor for worsening

---

## Fall / Season Close (3–4 hours)

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection — photograph and log, same protocol as spring open
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints before winter — moisture under ice is the worst sealant failure scenario
- [ ] Walk all four sidewalls — any new delamination bubbles? Soft spots?
- [ ] **Log your fall seal work: date, locations, product names, photos**

### Trailer Winterization

**Option 1 — Air Blow-Out:**
- [ ] Attach compressor to city water inlet via blow-out plug
- [ ] Max 50 PSI — do not exceed
- [ ] Open each faucet in sequence from low points up: outside shower → bathroom → kitchen
- [ ] Hold each open until air flow is consistent with no water mist
- [ ] Flush toilet, hold handle open until dry
- [ ] Drain fresh, gray, and black tanks fully
- [ ] Open low-point drain valves — leave open during storage

**Option 2 — Non-Toxic Antifreeze:**
- [ ] Drain all tanks
- [ ] Bypass water heater (do NOT put antifreeze through the water heater)
- [ ] Pour non-toxic pink RV antifreeze into fresh tank or use hand pump via city water port
- [ ] Run each faucet until pink antifreeze appears — kitchen, bathroom sinks, shower
- [ ] Pour antifreeze directly into toilet and all drains for P-traps
- [ ] Pour antifreeze in outdoor shower line if equipped
- [ ] Remove and store water filter cartridge inside

### Slide and Rubber Seal Preparation

- [ ] Apply 303 Aerospace to all rubber slide seals before retracting for winter
- [ ] Clean slide tracks, apply dry-lube
- [ ] Retract all slides for winter storage

### Battery and Electrical

- [ ] Connect a smart battery maintainer (not trickle charger) — maintains charge without overcharging
- [ ] If disconnecting batteries: store in temperature-regulated space if possible
- [ ] Verify solar panels are clean before winter — bird debris reduces any passive charging
- [ ] Cover A/C shrouds with ventilated covers — prevents debris and nesting

### Exterior and Tires

- [ ] Apply UV protectant (303 Aerospace) to all rubber exterior moldings, entry door seal, and slide seals before they retract
- [ ] Cover tires with UV-rated tire covers if storing outdoors
- [ ] Move trailer once monthly during extended storage to prevent tire flat spots
- [ ] Cover A/C units — shroud covers prevent debris and animals from nesting in the coils

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months)

- [ ] Wheel bearing inspection and repack (packed bearings) or inspection (sealed)
- [ ] Brake inspection — pad wear, magnet condition, drum wear
- [ ] Lug nut torque — re-torque after first 50 miles of season and again at 12 months
- [ ] Smoke detector battery replacement
- [ ] CO and propane detector test — replace if past rated service life (5–7 years)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher: verify charge, inspect pin and nozzle
- [ ] A/C coil cleaning: spray coil cleaner on evaporator coils (do not use pressure water)
- [ ] Water heater anode rod inspection (LP/electric combo heaters — replace when more than 75% consumed)
- [ ] Roof vent crank mechanism: inspect and lubricate with dry lubricant

## The Maintenance Log

One entry per event:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product name, specific location)
5. Photos attached

Grand Design's structural and frame warranties (3-year and 5-year) require you to maintain the unit in a "workmanlike manner." A maintenance log with dated seal photos is your best protection if a warranty claim is disputed on grounds of "inadequate maintenance." It's also a genuine resale differentiator — the 1-3-5 transferable warranty means the next buyer can see exactly what was maintained.
