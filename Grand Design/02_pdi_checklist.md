# Grand Design Imagine — PDI Checklist
*2970RL · 2810BH · 2800BH*
*Date: 2026-05-23*

Print this. Bring it to the dealership. Do not sign until every item is checked.

**Time budget: 3–4 hours minimum.** Grand Design ships units through a factory 300-point inspection, but documented defects still reach buyers. The factory inspection does not replace your own.

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Factory-Documented Defects (Start Here)

These are the highest-risk items documented across MyGrandRV.com, iRV2, and GDRVowners.com. Test these before anything else.

### 1. Bunk Room Sliding Door — 2810BH & 2800BH
**Known defect:** Missing or improperly seated floor guide stops allow the bunk room sliding door to jump off track on first use.

- [ ] Slide the bunk room door fully open and fully closed at least 3 times
- [ ] Push the door laterally (side-to-side) while it's on the track — it should not flex out of the guide
- [ ] Confirm floor guide stops are present and seated at both ends of the travel path
- [ ] If door pops off track or binds: **do not accept the unit until this is repaired and retested**

---

### 2. Gray/Black Tank Dump Valve Labels — All Models
**Known defect:** Gray and black tank dump valve labels have been swapped at the factory on documented 2023–2025 units.

- [ ] Ask the service tech to run fresh water through the black tank flush valve
- [ ] Pull the valve labeled "black" and confirm black tank drains
- [ ] Pull the valve labeled "gray" and confirm gray tank drains
- [ ] If labels are swapped: note on PDI form, have corrected or re-labeled before delivery

---

### 3. GFCI / Circuit Loading — All Models
**Known defect:** Interior outlets are daisy-chained on a single GFCI circuit. Running two appliances simultaneously trips the circuit.

- [ ] Confirm shore power is connected
- [ ] Plug a microwave and coffee maker into different outlets and run both simultaneously
- [ ] Verify no GFCI trip, no breaker trip
- [ ] Trip the master GFCI outlet and verify the correct downstream outlets go dead — confirms wiring sequence
- [ ] Reset and verify all outlets restore

---

### 4. Roof Membrane Adhesion — All Models (especially 2970RL)
**Known defect:** TPO rubber roof membrane can lift at corners or rear wall-to-roof transition on first road trip. Trim also documented pulling from sidewall.

- [ ] Get on the roof — Imagine has a walkable roof, use a 6-foot ladder
- [ ] Press along all four corners where the roof membrane meets the front cap and rear wall
- [ ] Press along the full perimeter where the roof edge meets the sidewall trim
- [ ] Any lifting, bubbling, or separating membrane: note and photograph before signing
- [ ] Inspect all roof vent collars, A/C gaskets, and antenna seals — press with fingernail, looking for cracking or separation
- [ ] **2970RL specific:** the rear wall-to-roof transition is the highest-risk spot — press the full length of the rear cap seam

---

### 5. Refrigerator Cold Function — All Models
**Known defect:** Documented units where refrigerator never cooled from day one. Some were shipped with gas line contamination preventing proper burner operation.

- [ ] Confirm fridge was running on shore power at least 4 hours before your PDI began
- [ ] Check internal temperature with a thermometer — must be below 40°F
- [ ] Switch to propane mode; verify flame lights and unit holds temperature
- [ ] If fridge is at ambient temp after 4+ hours: **do not accept until service department diagnoses and resolves**

---

## Exterior Walk

### Roof (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk the full roof — one-piece TPO should be smooth, no bubbles or wrinkles
- [ ] Inspect all penetrations: A/C units (typically 2 on these floor plans), vents, MaxxAir fans, solar panel brackets if equipped
- [ ] Every sealant joint: press with fingernail — should be pliable, not cracked or crumbling
- [ ] Re-inspect the four corners at the roof edge (highest failure risk for TPO membrane)
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint from the roof — date-stamp photos before leaving the lot**

### Sidewalls and Ends

- [ ] Walk all four sides — look for delamination bubbles, stress cracks, or panel separation
- [ ] Press along all vertical seams, corner caps, and window trim
- [ ] Check the front cap fiberglass: any cracks around the A-frame or hitch area?
- [ ] Inspect rear corner caps fully — these are documented road damage collection points
- [ ] Check all compartment door hinges and latches — every door should close flush and latch without force

### Entry Door and Windows

- [ ] Open and close main entry door — should latch cleanly with no binding
- [ ] Screen door closes and latches without gaps
- [ ] All windows open, close, and lock — check screen condition
- [ ] Frameless windows (if equipped): check seal gaskets around perimeter — press gently to verify no air gaps

---

## Water System

- [ ] Shore power and city water connected before this section
- [ ] **Connect city water slowly — walk entire fresh water system under pressure before cranking to full flow**
- [ ] Open every faucet (kitchen, bathroom, outside shower if equipped) and look beneath each for drips
- [ ] Check all cabinet bases under sinks for water stains or soft spots — indicators of prior leaks
- [ ] Run shower: check for floor leaks at pan edges and door seals
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank: prime and verify pressure, check for any drips at pump connections
- [ ] Flush all three toilets — confirm bowl clears and no water runs constantly after flush
- [ ] Check gray and black dump valves per the GFCI/label check above
- [ ] Run tank flush (black tank rinse if equipped)

---

## Slides — All Models

- [ ] Extend all slides fully — they should travel smoothly with no grinding or hesitation
- [ ] Walk the interior with all slides out — check floor seams for gaps or lifting
- [ ] Inspect rubber wiper seals on all four sides of each slide: no tears, no gaps
- [ ] Retract all slides — smooth operation, seals compress evenly
- [ ] Re-run each slide once more and look for any error codes on the Schwintek controller display (if visible)

---

## Electrical and Solar

- [ ] Test every interior light switch
- [ ] Test every 12V outlet (USB panels, etc.)
- [ ] Test every 120V outlet with a plug tester
- [ ] GFCI test: trip and reset the master GFCI; verify correct downstream outlets respond
- [ ] Check the battery bank: verify state of charge, confirm all batteries are equal charge
- [ ] **If solar is installed:** verify MPPT controller shows PV input and charging current on a sunny day
- [ ] Check the inverter/charger display — no error codes

---

## Appliances

- [ ] **Refrigerator:** confirmed cold (see CRITICAL section)
- [ ] **Microwave:** heat a cup of water for 60 seconds
- [ ] **Cooktop:** light all burners, confirm clean ignition, all hold flame
- [ ] **Oven:** fire, hold 350°F for 10 minutes
- [ ] **Range hood:** all speeds, confirm exhaust direction
- [ ] **Furnace:** run a full heat cycle, verify warm air at all heat registers — check for spider nests in orifices after storage
- [ ] **A/C (all units):** run both units 5 minutes each, feel for cold air at every vent — 2970RL will have at least one rear and one front unit
- [ ] **Water heater:** run full cycle, verify hot at kitchen and bath

---

## Interior

- [ ] Push on every wall-mounted component: TV brackets, shelving, towel bars, paper holders
- [ ] Open every drawer and cabinet door — slides should glide smoothly, nothing should bind
- [ ] **Check countertops for bubbling or warping** — documented on Imagine line; run your palm flat across entire surface
- [ ] Bunk beds: climb on each bunk, rock it — ladders should be solid, mattress supports should not flex excessively
- [ ] Check under sink cabinets: no sawdust, no loose wire nuts, no unsealed plumbing penetrations
- [ ] Inspect the floor throughout — no soft spots, no squeaking on first walk

---

## Chassis and Underside

- [ ] Walk the underside with a flashlight: look for any obvious corrosion on the frame
- [ ] Verify all tank drain lines are connected and not kinked
- [ ] Check breakaway cable is connected and the pin is pulled — confirm trailer brakes engage when pin is removed
- [ ] Verify all running lights and brake lights work (test with your tow vehicle connected)
- [ ] Inspect all tires: date codes, tread condition, sidewall cracking
- [ ] Torque lug nuts after PDI before leaving — or confirm dealer has done this within 24 hours

---

## Before You Sign — Non-Negotiables

- [ ] **Signed PDI form** with your name, date, and every item tested noted
- [ ] **"Fix before delivery" items** resolved in full — or scheduled in writing with a specific date
- [ ] **Dated photos of every exterior seal** on all four sides and roof taken before the unit moves
- [ ] **Full warranty documentation** in hand — 1-year base, 3-year structural, 5-year frame
- [ ] **Service department direct contact** — not the sales desk. Name, phone, email.
- [ ] **Register with Grand Design on delivery day** at [granddesignrv.com](https://www.granddesignrv.com/owners/registration)
- [ ] **Gray/black valve labels verified correct** — write which is which on the PDI form even if correct at delivery

---

## Print-and-Bring Summary — 5 Critical Tests

Before anything else, on the day of PDI:

1. **Bunk door** (2800BH / 2810BH): open/close 3×, push laterally — stays on track?
2. **Dump valves**: flush test each tank, confirm labels match actual tanks
3. **GFCI circuit**: run microwave + coffee maker simultaneously — no trip?
4. **Roof membrane**: get on the roof, press all four corners and rear wall seam — no lift?
5. **Refrigerator**: thermometer inside, must be below 40°F after 4+ hours on shore power

**If any of these five fail: do not sign. Every issue is the dealer's cost before the keys change hands. After signing, it's a warranty claim.**
