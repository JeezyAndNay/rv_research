# Alliance RV Delta 281BH & 284RK - Recurring Seasonal Maintenance Schedule
*Date: 2026-05-22*

---

## Why This Schedule Exists

Alliance excludes water damage caused by "inadequate seal maintenance" even when a factory defect initiated the breach. That language puts the burden on you to prove you maintained the seals. This schedule is designed so that if Alliance ever invokes that exclusion, you have a dated paper trail showing exactly what you did and when.

**Every seal inspection gets photographed and logged. Every product gets documented.**

---

## Supplies to Keep On Hand Year-Round

| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| Dicor 501LSW self-leveling lap sealant | Horizontal roof surfaces |
| Dicor 505LSW non-sag lap sealant | Vertical sidewall seams |
| Dicor RP-RC roof cleaner | Clean before re-sealing |
| 303 Aerospace Protectant | Slide rubber seal conditioning |
| Protect All slide-out seal treatment | Alternative to 303 for slide seals |
| Dielectric grease | Electrical connections |
| Plumber's grease (silicone-based) | Tank valve O-rings |
| Stabilizer jack lubricant | Jack maintenance |
| **Dated maintenance log** | **Warranty defense documentation** |

## Before Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk all four sides - eyes on every roof-to-wall seam, corner seams, slide topper edges. Look for anything that changed since last trip
- [ ] Check all exterior compartment latches close and lock
- [ ] Verify propane tanks secured in enclosure with latch set
- [ ] Run each slide out and back in once - verify clean operation before leaving
- [ ] Safety chain and breakaway cable connected, all lights working

---

## After Every Trip (15 minutes)

- [ ] Walk exterior again - look for new damage from road debris, low branches, backing incidents
- [ ] Check slide rubber seals for debris trapped in them - remove before it sits
- [ ] If drove in rain: check roof when dry for standing water indicating drainage issue
- [ ] Note anything that rattled, malfunctioned, or seemed off while fresh

---

## Spring / Season Open (3-4 hours)
*Most important maintenance event of the year*

### Roof & Seals - Annual Inspection and Documentation

- [ ] **Get on the roof** - inspect every lap sealant joint: both A/C unit bases (all four sides each), all four corners of each roof vent, antenna base, solar panel brackets (281BH), all penetrations
- [ ] **Photograph every sealant joint on the roof - every single one, dated**
  - This is your warranty defense documentation
- [ ] **Test every joint** - press fingernail into sealant. Good sealant has slight give; cracking or pulling away from surface needs replacement
- [ ] Re-seal any compromised joints with Dicor 501LSW - clean area with mineral spirits first, let dry, apply overlapping old sealant by 1 inch each side, cure 24 hours before rain
- [ ] **Photograph repaired joints and log: what was resealed, product name, date**

**Exterior wall seals:**
- [ ] Walk all four sides at eye level - inspect every panel seam, corner cap joint, seal around exterior lights, vents, cable/antenna entry points
- [ ] Press-test all seams - re-apply Dicor 505LSW to anything showing separation
- [ ] **Photograph and log all work**

**Slide topper and rubber wiper seals:**
- [ ] Extend slide(s) and inspect rubber wiper seal along top and sides - look for cracking, tears, separation from slide frame
- [ ] Condition rubber seals with 303 Aerospace Protectant or Protect All - prevents cracking that leads to water intrusion. Never use petroleum-based products on rubber seals
- [ ] Inspect slide membrane (large flat seal on top of slide) for tears or separations
- [ ] Retract slide and check where slide wall meets main wall from inside - any daylight visible is a seal issue

### De-Winterization

- [ ] Flush antifreeze from all lines - open every faucet until water runs clear
- [ ] Reconnect and inspect water filter
- [ ] Re-pressurize system - 30 minutes city water pressure, complete visual check of every connection
- [ ] Test water pump on fresh tank water
- [ ] Re-install any drain plugs pulled for winterization
- [ ] **Verify water heater bypass valves are returned to normal flow-through position before running hot water**

**281BH solar:**
- [ ] Verify MPPT charge controller shows correct panel input after winter storage
- [ ] Confirm battery bank voltage and solar system charging normally

### Electrical

- [ ] Test every 12V and 110V outlet
- [ ] **Check all Azdel-mounted components after winter thermal cycling** - freeze-thaw expansion can work screws loose. Push on every shelf, TV mount, and wall bracket
- [ ] Inspect coax connections - re-tighten any that have worked loose
- [ ] Apply dielectric grease to exterior electrical connections

### Appliances - Annual Start-Up

- [ ] **A/C units:** run both for 20 minutes before first hot trip - verify both compressors engage and produce cold air
- [ ] **Furnace:** fire for one full heat cycle even in spring - confirms ignitor, limit switch, and blower survived winter
- [ ] **Water heater:** run a full cycle, check for proper ignition and temperature
- [ ] **Refrigerator:** run on electric for 24 hours, verify reaches and holds below 40°F
- [ ] **Cooktop:** light all three burners - winter can cause spider nests in burner orifices
- [ ] Inspect LP regulator and hoses for cracks - rubber degrades over winter

### Chassis & Tires

- [ ] Check tire pressure cold before any trip
- [ ] Inspect tire sidewalls for cracking - UV/ozone exposure causes crazing year-round
- [ ] Check date codes (DOT code - last 4 digits = week and year of manufacture). Replace tires over 5-6 years regardless of tread
- [ ] Grease hitch ball and coupler
- [ ] Check lug nut torque (typically 110-120 ft-lbs - verify your spec)

## Mid-Summer Check (1-2 hours)

Mid-season is when UV damage accumulates and marginal spring issues have had time to become real problems.

**Roof - mid-season inspection:**
- [ ] Get on roof - specifically check seals that were borderline at spring open
- [ ] Check solar panel mounting brackets (281BH) - thermal expansion cycles can loosen fasteners
- [ ] Check PVC roof covering for lifting at edges or punctures

**Slide seal condition check:**
- [ ] Condition rubber wiper seals again - summer heat dries them out; second application warranted
- [ ] Extend all slides (284RK: both) and inspect membrane seals

**Azdel component check:**
- [ ] Push on every mounted component - mid-season check for Azdel screw pull-through
- [ ] Specifically: TV bracket, shelf under TV (284RK), bunk ladder mounts (281BH), towel bars, toilet paper holders

**Underbelly inspection:**
- [ ] Look under trailer for sagging underbelly material (indicates water intrusion into insulation) or hanging water lines
- [ ] Check all underbelly access panels are sealed and secured

---

## Fall / Season Close (3-4 hours)

**Do the seal inspection BEFORE winterization.** Sequence matters - seal against the coming winter, not close over active damage.

### Final Seal Inspection Before Winter

- [ ] Full roof inspection as described in Spring - photograph and log everything
- [ ] Re-apply lap sealant to any joints showing any degradation during the season
- [ ] **Document fall seal work:**
  - Dated photos of every sealed joint
  - Log entry: "Fall season close [date] - inspected and resealed [specific joints]. Products used: [product names]"
  - This log entry covers your warranty for the entire following year

### Winterization

**Option 1: Compressed air blow-out**
- [ ] Connect air compressor (max 50 PSI) to city water inlet
- [ ] Open each faucet individually and blow until no water comes out - start with low points first
- [ ] Drain all three tanks first, then blow

**Option 2: RV antifreeze (non-toxic pink only)**
- [ ] Use only non-toxic RV antifreeze (pink), never automotive antifreeze
- [ ] Run through system until pink comes out each faucet
- [ ] **Bypass water heater before winterizing** - do not fill with antifreeze
- [ ] Pour antifreeze directly into toilet bowl and down each drain to protect P-traps

**Both methods:**
- [ ] Remove and bring inside the water filter cartridge - freezing destroys filter media
- [ ] Open low-point drain valves and leave open during storage

**Appliances:**
- [ ] Defrost and clean refrigerator - leave door propped open 2 inches during storage. Remove all food
- [ ] Clean cooktop thoroughly - spider nest prevention
- [ ] Shut off propane at tank valves

**Slides:**
- [ ] Retract all slides for storage
- [ ] Apply slide seal conditioner to rubber wiper seals before retracting
- [ ] Clean slide rails and apply dry lubricant (not WD-40)

**Electrical:**
- [ ] Connect battery maintainer (not trickle charger) to keep battery bank healthy, or disconnect batteries and store inside
- [ ] 281BH: if in freezing temps, verify MPPT is set for winter charging if leaving connected

**Tires:**
- [ ] Move trailer once a month if possible during storage to prevent flat spots
- [ ] If not possible, put tire cradles under wheels to distribute weight
- [ ] Cover tires with UV covers if stored outdoors

**Exterior:**
- [ ] Apply UV protectant to all exterior rubber moldings and corner caps
- [ ] Cover A/C shrouds to prevent debris and nesting

## Annual Service Items (Every 12 Months Regardless of Use)

- [ ] A/C unit service - clean filters monthly during use; clean coils annually with A/C coil cleaner
- [ ] Roof vent inspection and lubrication - crank mechanism corrodes over time
- [ ] Re-torque lug nuts after first 50 miles of season and again at 12 months
- [ ] Inspect and grease all hinge points on storage compartment doors
- [ ] Check all exterior lights - replace cracked or yellowed lenses (water gets in through cracks)
- [ ] Test CO and propane detector - replace unit if past rated service life (typically 5-7 years; date on unit)
- [ ] Test smoke detector - replace battery
- [ ] Inspect fire extinguisher charge level

## The Maintenance Log - How to Keep It

One entry per maintenance event containing:
1. Date
2. What you inspected
3. What you found (including "nothing to report")
4. What you did (product names, specific joints resealed)
5. Photos attached

**If Alliance ever invokes the "inadequate seal maintenance" exclusion, you're presenting:**
- Dated inspection log showing biannual inspections minimum
- Photo evidence of seal condition at each inspection
- Product documentation showing appropriate sealant
- Repair log showing you addressed degradation promptly

That's a strong defense. An owner who can produce nothing has a much weaker position.
