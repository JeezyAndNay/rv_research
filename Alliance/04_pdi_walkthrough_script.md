# Alliance RV Delta 281BH & 284RK - PDI Walkthrough Script
*Word-for-word script for use at the dealership*
*Date: 2026-05-22*

**Time budget: plan for 3-4 hours minimum.** If the dealer says "we usually do these in an hour," say:
> *"I appreciate that - I just want to make sure we have time to run everything through its paces. I'm happy to stay as long as it takes."*

---

## Before You Arrive

Call the dealer the day before:

> *"I want to make sure the unit is connected to shore power and the refrigerator has been running for at least four hours before I get there. I also want it plugged into city water when I arrive. Can you make sure that's set up? I want to be able to test everything fully during the walkthrough."*

**If they push back:** yellow flag. A dealer who won't pre-stage the unit for testing doesn't have confidence in it.

---

## Arrival - Set the Frame (5 minutes)

> *"I want to be upfront - I've done my homework on the Delta and I have a pretty thorough list of things I want to test today. I'm not trying to make your day hard, I just want to make sure we catch anything that needs to go back to your service department before I drive home. Fair?"*

Then:

> *"One thing I need before we start - can I get a copy of the PDI form your team filled out? And I'll need a signed copy at the end with anything we find today added to it."*

**If they say "we don't really do it that way":**
> *"I understand - I just want a written record of the condition at delivery. It protects both of us. Even a simple handwritten note on dealer letterhead works."*

**If they won't provide any written documentation:** hard red flag.

---

## Solar System - START HERE FIRST (281BH) (15 minutes)

Walk to the pass-through storage bay:

> *"I'd like to start with the solar system before we go inside. I've read that some 281BH owners had the solar leads wired incorrectly at the factory - to one battery instead of in series. Can you show me how the batteries are wired?"*

**What you want to see:** two 6V batteries connected positive-to-negative in series to create a 12V bank, with solar leads going to the system, not directly to one battery.

If the dealer says they already checked it:
> *"Great - can you show me? I just want to see it myself."*

Then:
> *"Can we check the output at the MPPT charge controller? On a sunny day it should show around 44 volts coming in from the panels."*

**If the PV input shows 0.0V on a clear day with unshaded panels:** you've found the defect. Do not accept the unit until corrected and retested.

> *"I've also read that the positive PV line has a butt-connector splice behind the electrical panel. Can we take a look at that and make sure it's solid?"*

---

## Slides (15 minutes)
*284RK: 2 slides | 281BH: 1 slide*

> *"Let's run the slide out. I want to cycle it a few times - three full in-and-out cycles."*

While it's moving, watch and listen. Then:

> *"After it's fully extended, can you walk the slide floor edges with me? I've seen reports of the flooring pulling up at the edges on some units."*

Run your foot along every seam where slide floor meets main floor. If you find a lip:
> *"This edge isn't flush - can you flag this on the PDI form? I'd like service to address it before I take delivery."*

Then:
> *"Now I want to check the wall mounting on that shelf under the TV."* (284RK) or *"I want to check all the wall-mounted components in here."*

Push gently outward on every shelf.

> *"Now I want to try reaching the tank dump handles with the slide out. Can you show me where they are?"*

For the 284RK, repeat slide cycling and floor-edge check for both slides.

---

## City Water & Plumbing (20 minutes)

> *"Is the unit connected to city water right now? I'd like to walk it fully pressurized."*

If not connected, ask them to connect it now. Then:

> *"I'm going to open every cabinet under every sink and check for drips while we're pressurized. Can you follow me through and we'll go room by room?"*

Then:
> *"Now let's run the shower. I want to run it for three full minutes and then check the bathroom floor outside the shower pan. This is a documented leak point on Delta models."*

Run shower, turn on kitchen faucet simultaneously. After 3 minutes, check floor outside pan.

> *"Can you run through the individual fixture shutoffs with me? Alliance makes a point of advertising those - I want to verify each one actually works."*

Then:
> *"I'd like to test the tank dump handles - can we walk through operating them?"*

If any handle pulls but nothing moves at the valve: that's the documented "not hooked up" defect.

---

## Monitor Panel - 281BH (10 minutes)

> *"I want to go through the monitor panel with you. I've read that the 2026 281BH has two grey tank buttons and the manual doesn't explain the layout clearly. Can you walk me through every button and tell me what each one monitors?"*

Take notes or photos of whatever they explain.

---

## Appliances (20 minutes)

**Both A/C units:**
> *"Both A/C units are standard - can we turn them both on and let them run? I want to verify both compressors kick on and each one is reaching cold air."*

Wait at least 5 minutes. Put your hand at each vent and feel for actual cold air.

**Cooktop:**
> *"Let's light all three burners."*

Watch each ignitor click, confirm each flame holds.

**Oven:**
> *"Can we turn the oven on to 350 and verify it's actually heating?"*

**Refrigerator:**
> *"Is the fridge on and has it been running since this morning? What temperature is it reading?"*

Should be below 40°F after 4+ hours.

**Range hood:**
> *"Fan on all speeds, please - and verify it's actually venting outside, not just recirculating."*

**Awning:**
> *"Let's deploy the full awning and retract it."*

---

## Exterior Seals & Roof (20 minutes)

> *"I want to do a walk of the roof before we wrap up - it's a walk-on roof, so that's fine. Can we get a ladder?"*

Walk the full roof. Look at every lap sealant joint around both A/C units, all four vent corners, antenna base.

> *"I'm going to photograph every exterior seal on all four sides before I drive away. Is there anything you want to point out to me?"*

> *"What's Alliance's definition of 'inadequate seal maintenance' in the warranty? I want to understand exactly what my obligation is."*

If they don't know, ask for the warranty document to read before signing.

---

## Walkthrough Wrap-Up (Before Signing)

> *"Here's everything I want on the PDI form before I sign."*

Read off your list of findings. Then:

> *"For anything you can fix today, I want it fixed and retested before I take the unit. For anything that needs to be scheduled, I want it written on the form as a 'fix before delivery' item - meaning I'll take delivery after it's resolved, not before."*

---

## Handling "We'll Fix It After You Sign"

Dealers often say: *"We'll take care of that - just sign today and bring it back next week."*

Your response:
> *"I appreciate that. I'd rather not take delivery until it's resolved - can we schedule that and I'll come back? Or if it can be fixed today, I'm happy to wait."*

Once you sign and drive away, every issue becomes a warranty claim. Issues on the PDI form before signing are the dealer's responsibility at their cost.

If dealer pushes hard:
> *"I've been looking forward to this - I just want to start ownership on the right foot. I'm not trying to be difficult. Can we talk to your service manager about a realistic timeline?"*

---

## Final Checklist Before Keys Change Hands

- [ ] Signed PDI form in hand with all findings noted
- [ ] Any "fix before delivery" items resolved or formally scheduled with written commitment date
- [ ] Full warranty document reviewed - especially water damage exclusion
- [ ] Alliance warranty registered or dealer confirms they register at delivery
- [ ] Service department direct contact info - name, phone, email
- [ ] Dealer confirmed they have Alliance-authorized technicians
- [ ] All owner's manuals, remote controls, keys, and hardware in hand
- [ ] Monitor panel explained and understood
- [ ] Serial number and VIN confirmed to match paperwork

Then ask:
> *"Before I sign - just want to confirm: if I find something within the first 30 days that appears to be a factory defect, what's your process? Who do I call and what's the typical turnaround for getting parts?"*

Listen carefully. The answer tells you exactly what your post-purchase experience will look like.

---

## If the Dealer Says No to Any of This

Polite version:
> *"I completely understand this is unusual - I just want to make sure we're both protected. A thorough PDI is good for your service department too, since it means I won't be back next week with a list of things to fix."*

Firm version:
> *"I'm not comfortable signing without completing the inspection. I'd rather reschedule for when the unit is ready to be fully tested. Can we do that?"*

A dealer who won't let you run city water pressure, cycle the slides, or provide written documentation is telling you the unit isn't ready. You have full leverage before you sign. None of it after.
