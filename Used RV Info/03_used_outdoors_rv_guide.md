# Used Outdoors RV Buyer's Guide
*BackCountry · Timber Ridge · Blackstone — Mountain Series & Titanium Series*
*Research date: 2026-05-22 | For personal use only*

---

## Should You Buy a Used Outdoors RV?

**Short answer: yes, if you have the tow vehicle and the budget for one.**

Outdoors RV Manufacturing in La Grande, Oregon builds a genuinely different product than the Indiana mega-factory brands. Owner community consensus on [iRV2](https://www.irv2.com/forums/f282/) is consistent across years: these trailers are built better than 75% of what's on the market, hold up under hard use, and look good after 100+ nights when equivalent trailers are showing wear. The known issues are real but manageable — they're mostly component selections (undersized jacks, wrong-size fittings) rather than structural failures. That's an important distinction when buying used.

**The catch:** ORV trailers are heavy, long, and expensive. Every floor plan in this guide requires a 3/4-ton minimum. The 28DBS loads to ~10,000 lbs at the road. If your tow vehicle can't handle it safely, move on. If it can, you're looking at one of the best used buys in the premium travel trailer market.

## Sub-Lines and Series — Know What You're Looking At

### Sub-Lines (Outdoors RV Brands)

| Sub-Line | Focus | Key Difference |
|---|---|---|
| **BackCountry** | Off-grid, boondocking, families | Generator standard on Titanium; dual gray tanks on 28DBS |
| **Timber Ridge** | Family camping, value tier | Lighter weight; documented awning leaks on older units |
| **Blackstone** | Premium couples/entertaining | Longest production history; most owner data available |

### Series (Construction Tiers)

| Series | Exterior | Auto Leveling | Windows | Price Premium |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mountain Series** | Aluminum skin | No | Standard double-pane | Base |
| **Titanium Series** | Lamilux HD 4000 fiberglass | Lippert Ground Control TT | Frameless thermal pane | +$7K–$10.7K |

**Buy Titanium if you can find it.** The Lamilux fiberglass holds up better long-term than aluminum skin, the frameless windows are a structural change you can't add later, and the Ground Control TT auto leveling is a meaningful quality-of-life upgrade at camp. The resale premium follows you when you sell.

If you find a Mountain Series unit at a significant discount, it's still a good buy — same chassis, suspension, and four-season construction, just without those three upgrades.

## What Years to Target

| Year Range | Verdict | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-2017 | Proceed carefully | Older Timber Ridge units showed frame rust. Inspect closely. Less documented used history |
| 2017–2019 | Acceptable | Titanium series established, features mature. Watch for Timber Ridge rust on lower-mileage units |
| 2020–2022 | Sweet spot | Post-refinement production, fewer rust reports, full Titanium feature set. Best used value window |
| 2023+ | Still depreciating | Buying close to new prices — price differential from new narrows |

The 28DBS has the richest owner community and the longest track record — it's the safest used buy if condition is comparable. The Blackstone 280RKS has years of production history and the most forum knowledge available for anything that goes wrong.

## Red Flags — Walk Away or Negotiate Hard

### Deal-Breakers

| Issue | Why |
|---|---|
| Delamination on fiberglass front cap | Lamilux is durable but a delaminated cap means moisture already got in. Repair is expensive and can hide larger structural damage |
| Soft spots on floor — any location | Moisture intrusion from above (roof/seam failure) or below. Investigate further before any offer |
| Frame rust (visible on undercarriage, especially Timber Ridge pre-2018) | Structural concern on a heavy trailer. Not always fatal but needs professional assessment |
| Slide that won't retract fully or binds | Could be seal damage, motor issue, or structural alignment — any of these are expensive |
| Black/gray water system non-functional | Tank valve, pump, or plumbing failure — negotiate for repair cost or walk |

### Major — Negotiate Price Down

| Issue | Estimated Repair Range |
|---|---|
| Auto leveling system errors / won't complete cycle | $300–1,200 (Lippert service) |
| Generator rough-running (spark plug gap or bigger) | $50–2,000 (plug gap fix to carburetor rebuild) |
| Roof sealant cracking / lap seal failure | $200–800 DIY / $500–2,000 pro |
| Awning seal leak (Timber Ridge) | $100–400 |
| Water fill line clamps wrong-size (blew off) | $20–50 in parts; labor if it caused downstream damage |
| Refrigerator not reaching temperature | $100–600 (burner cleaning to replacement) |

## Known Recurring Defects — Verify on Every Unit

These are documented factory defects found consistently across the line. Not "maybe" issues — verified and reported by owners across the iRV2, IFish, and RV Insider communities.

### 1. Water Fill Line Clamps (All Floor Plans)
Wrong-size clamps on water fill lines blow off on first city water connection. **Test:** connect city water slowly, walk every cabinet, under every sink, behind toilet. Run for 30 minutes at full pressure. If this hasn't happened yet on an older used unit, it may still blow. Keep replacement clamps (3/4"–1-1/4" assorted) on hand.

### 2. Black Tank Flush Valve Installed Backwards (28DBS Primary, Others Possible)
Documented on the 28DBS. The flush valve is installed so water runs the wrong direction. **Test:** run a tank flush and verify water is flowing into the tank in the correct direction. This is a field-fixable plumbing repair but verify it exists before buying.

### 3. Generator Spark Plug Gap Wrong (26RPS & 28DBS — Onan 3.6k)
Factory spec is .020". Documented units shipped with .028". The wrong gap causes rough running and, if not caught, later fuel system issues. **Test:** ask the seller to pull one spark plug. A correct gap is .020"; anything else is a flag. Ask when generator was last serviced and for records.

### 4. Stabilizer Jacks (All Floor Plans — Universal)
Stock ORV stabilizer jacks are consistently rated inadequate across all sub-lines and all price points. This is not a defect in the traditional sense — it's a known component decision. **Expect:** the unit will rock with stock jacks. If the seller has already replaced them with aftermarket units (X-Chocks, BAL, or Olympian), that's a plus. If not, budget $150–500 immediately.

### 5. Auto Leveling Re-Zero Needed (Titanium — All Floor Plans)
The Lippert Ground Control TT system commonly needs re-zeroing in the field after first use. The control head reports "batteries low" while the solar display shows full. **Test:** request a full auto-level cycle at the showing. If it doesn't complete without errors, ask why. This is usually a calibration fix, not a hardware failure — but verify.

## PDI / Pre-Purchase Inspection Checklist

If buying from a dealer and doing a PDI, or doing your own walkthrough on a private purchase:

### Must-Do Before Any Offer

- [ ] Auto level full cycle — complete without error (Titanium only)
- [ ] Generator 10-minute run + spark plug gap verified at .020" (26RPS / 28DBS)
- [ ] City water connected slow — walk entire unit for blown clamps, water at every fixture
- [ ] Black tank flush — verify flow direction (28DBS especially)
- [ ] Fridge at or below 40°F after 4+ hours on
- [ ] All slides — 3 full cycles, check floor edges and wiper seals
- [ ] Both A/C units to cold — compressor engages, cold air from vents
- [ ] Stabilizer jacks — deploy and assess (plan for replacement regardless)
- [ ] Exterior seal walk — fiberglass front cap seam, all four sides, roof penetrations
- [ ] Floor — moisture meter on bathroom floor, under slides, around windows
- [ ] Awning — deploy and inspect seal at wall junction (28BKS especially)

### Documentation to Request (Seller)

- Maintenance records (generator hours especially)
- Any warranty claim history
- PDI form from original delivery (if available)
- Spark plug replacement records for generator

## Bottom Line

Outdoors RV makes a legitimate product. The known defects are real but none are structural disasters — wrong-size clamps, a backwards flush valve, and undersized jacks are all fixable for under $500. That's a different category than the delamination, water intrusion, and frame failures that plague lower-tier brands.

The sweet spot is a 2020–2022 Titanium unit that's been maintained and shows service records. If the seller has replaced the stabilizer jacks and has generator hours documented, you're dealing with an owner who paid attention. If they can't tell you when the generator was last serviced and don't know the spark plug gap spec, assume it hasn't been done.

Price accordingly and walk through every PDI item above before you sign anything.
